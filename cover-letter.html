> Dear founder, hiring manager, or curious recruiter,

Most marketers optimise a funnel. A few build the system that decides which funnels are worth building in the first place. That's the game I play.

I've spent the last five years running growth for L1 blockchains, perpetual DEXs, prediction markets, and DeFi protocols. At Autonity I launched the Forecastathon and onboarded 2,500+ quant traders. At RFX I drove 70,000+ signups for a zkSync perp DEX alpha and stood up a 30+ KOL program. At Relative I built an 18,000-member community from zero. At Mozaic I led a four-person marketing team.

The numbers matter, but the pattern behind them matters more: I find narratives before they're obvious, turn them into positioning nobody else owns, and ship the campaigns and partnerships that compound.

// I don't just run campaigns. I build the intelligence systems that tell me which campaigns to run.

In the last year I've been turning that approach into code. I've shipped six AI-powered tools: a competitor move monitor, a narrative hijacking bot, a positioning matrix generator, a competitive deep-dive engine, an open-source Web3 job bot, and a full content-generation engine with a 25-pattern humanizer. All Python and React, all live, all replacing hours of manual work per week. This is what I do when I'm not doing marketing. I build things that make marketing smarter.

Here's what that means for you: you get a marketing lead who can also sit next to your founders and engineers. Someone who understands why a perp DEX ships spot trading next, why an L1 needs a settlement-layer narrative, why a prediction market is a funnel for quant traders and not a casino. Someone who will ship a tool by Friday when the intel you need doesn't exist yet.

I'm looking for a Marketing Lead or Head of Marketing role at a high-growth Web3 company, remote, Dubai, or Singapore. I'm most useful in places where the ground is still shifting, the narrative isn't set, and the team is small enough that marketing has to own outcomes, not decks.

If that sounds like you, let's talk.
